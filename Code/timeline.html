Juan Manuel Fangio

Five-time world champion in the 1950s, Fangio dominated the early years of F1 with his precision and calm style. Still holds the highest winning percentage in F1 history.

Jim Clark

A two-time world champion and one of the purest talents in F1 history. Known for his smooth driving and connection with Lotus in the 1960s.

Niki Lauda

Three-time world champion, known for his incredible comeback after a near-fatal crash in 1976. A symbol of determination and precision driving.

Ayrton Senna

Three-time world champion and a true icon of the sport, Senna was famous for his unmatched skill in wet conditions and passionate racing spirit.

Alain Prost

Four-time world champion nicknamed "The Professor" for his intellectual and strategic approach to racing, often in rivalry with Senna.

Michael Schumacher

Seven-time world champion who set new standards in F1. Dominated the early 2000s with Ferrari and became the benchmark for greatness.

Kimi Räikkönen

World champion in 2007, "The Iceman" was admired for his cool demeanor and raw speed, becoming one of the most beloved drivers of his era.

Lewis Hamilton

Seven-time world champion, tied with Schumacher. Known for his consistency, speed, and activism off-track, Hamilton reshaped modern F1.

Sebastian Vettel

Four-time world champion with Red Bull, Vettel was known for dominating the early 2010s and becoming a respected leader and environmental advocate.

Stirling Moss

Often called the greatest driver never to win a world title. Known for his sportsmanship and speed in the 1950s.

Graham Hill

The only driver to win the Triple Crown: Monaco GP, Indy 500, and Le Mans. A British icon of the 1960s.

Gilles Villeneuve

Famous for his fearless driving and passionate fan base. Remembered as a Ferrari legend despite never winning a title.

Nigel Mansell

World Champion in 1992 with a bold driving style and immense popularity in Britain.

Nico Rosberg

Won the 2016 World Championship, beating teammate Hamilton and retiring shortly after.
